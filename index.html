Great Barrier Reef

The Great Barrier reef is the largest coral reef in all the world. It is over 3000 miles long from the North End to its southern end, off the West Coast of Australia. It is home to many species of fish plants and undersea wildlife that are nowhere else in all the world. Unfortunately, many of these lifeforms are under threat due to changes in ocean temperature resulting from climate change due to global warming.

Time is running out for opportunities to see this great wonder of the world. Although many parts of the Great Barrier reef are fluorescing and dying, there still remain many beautiful parts and portions to visit. It still remains one of the greatest places in the world to explore with scuba equipment.

Before You Get Here

There are many things to consider before you come to Australia and visit the Great Barrier reef. Make sure, first of all, that you are a good swimmer and that you have some experience scuba diving. Try visiting your local swim facilities to see if they offer training. It might also be a good idea to purchase your own equipment. That way you are familiar with your set and are able to adjust its settings to your preferences. Remember however, you cannot take pressurized tanks on the plane, but don't worry we will supply them here. So bring everything except those.
